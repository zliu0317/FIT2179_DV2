Section 01: The Big Picture

From 288,000 to 845,000 in 20 Years

Australia's international student population has nearly tripled since 2005. COVID-19 triggered the sharpest collapse on record, yet by 2024 enrolments hit an all-time high.

Total International Student Enrolments in Australia between 2005–2025
Key insight: COVID-19 closed Australia's borders in March 2020. Enrolments dropped 24% over two years, then rebounded faster than any prior recovery. The 2024 peak of 850,921 surpassed the pre-pandemic high by over 12%.
Education as Australia's 4th-Largest Export in 2024–25
Key insight: Education contributed AUD $53.6B to Australia's economy in 2024–25, ranking as the 4th-largest export. International students pay tuition fees around three times higher than domestic students, making each enrolment a significant economic contribution.
Domestic vs. Overseas Student Enrolments between 2015–2024
Key insight: By 2024, overseas students reached nearly 54% of the domestic total, showing how internationally embedded Australia's higher education has become.

Section 03: Where They Study

Victoria and NSW host two-thirds

International students are concentrated in Australia's two largest states. The Group of Eight universities attract the most students and are most financially dependent on their fees.

Student Density by State in 2024

Key insight: Normalised by population, the ACT has the highest concentration of international students relative to residents, followed closely by Victoria.
Comparison of Total and International Enrolments in Top 8 Universities in Australia by State in 2024
Key insight: International and total enrolments are strongly proportional. Universities with more total students also enrol more international students. University of Sydney and Monash University lead in absolute international student headcount. International students make up roughly 45–51% of total enrolments at these two universities. ANU and the University of Adelaide have smaller overall cohorts but still maintain around 33–34% international student share.
Top 8 University Revenue Breakdown in 2023

International students are far more profitable than domestic ones

Key insight: International student fees are the dominant and most profitable revenue stream for Australia's top 8 universities in 2023, dwarfing domestic student funding and often rivaling or exceeding "Other Revenue" streams, creating a heavy reliance on international enrolments for institutional income.
Section 04: What They Study

Fields of Study and Graduate Skills

Management and Commerce is the most popular field at every level. Information Technology has seen the fastest growth, up 22% in a single year.

International Students by Field and Level of Course in 2024
Key insight: Management dominates at Bachelor and Postgraduate Coursework levels, over 187,000 students across all levels. IT enrolments are heavily postgraduate, reflecting demand for skilled tech workers seeking migration pathways. Health is concentrated at Bachelor level. Creative Arts and Architecture remain niche fields. Sub-Bachelor enrolments are small across all disciplines, suggesting most students enter at Bachelor level or above. The dominance of professional and STEM fields reflects students' strategic focus on careers and visa eligibility.
